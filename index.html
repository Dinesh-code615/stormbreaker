High Tension Transmission Lines Fault Detector

Smart real-time fault detection for safer power transmission

Introduction

Transmission lines are the backbone of electrical power distribution, carrying high voltage electricity over long distances. Faults such as wire breakage, fire hazards, and power outages can cause severe disruptions, safety risks, and economic losses.

Detecting faults quickly is crucial to prevent accidents and ensure reliable power supply. This project aims to detect faults in real time using sensors and alert mechanisms, improving safety and efficiency in power transmission.

System Overview

Two endpoints are connected by three copper transmission lines. The system uses thermistor sensors, a dual channel relay, and a 240V AC supply with a regulator to monitor and indicate faults.

Working Principle

Normal Condition → No bulbs lit
Fire Fault → Thermistor detects heat → Relay activates → Bulb lights up
Wire Break → Relay detects loss → Bulb lights up

Components

Thermistor Sensor: Detects heat due to overload or fire.
Dual Channel Relay: Activates bulbs for fault indication.
Bulbs: Indicate which line has a fault.
Voltage Regulator & AC Supply: Ensures safe operation at 240V AC.

Advantages

  • Real-time fault detection
  • Simple and cost-effective
  • Suitable for rural and industrial areas
  • Improves safety and reliability
  • Future expansion: wireless alerts or auto-shutdown

Conclusion

This project demonstrates a practical and scalable solution for monitoring high tension transmission lines using simple electronics.
